Privacy

Synapse · last updated July 2026

Synapse is a free game with no ads, no analytics, no tracking pixels, and nothing sold to anyone. This page describes everything the game stores and why.

What is stored when you create an account

  • Email address — your sign-in identity; also how friends find you to send challenges and friend requests. Verified once by a confirmation link.
  • Display name and country — shown to the people you play.
  • Age band (for example “25–34”) — used to normalize the Synapse Score within your age group. Your exact date of birth is used once, on your device, to confirm you're 18 or over; it is not kept.
  • Avatar — your chosen character or an uploaded picture.
  • Gameplay data — rating, wins, per-domain accuracy, recent duels, daily-challenge scores, and which questions you've seen recently.
  • Friends and challenges — who you've added, and open invitations.

Chat is end-to-end encrypted

Messages between friends are encrypted on your device (ECDH + AES-GCM via your browser's Web Crypto). The server stores only ciphertext it cannot read. Your chat key is protected by your account password; resetting the password starts a fresh key, and old messages become unreadable.

Where the data lives

Accounts and game data are stored in Google Firebase (Authentication and Cloud Firestore), hosted in the United States, under Google's data-processing terms. The game itself is a static site; it sets no advertising cookies and loads no third-party trackers or fonts.

What other players can see

People you play or befriend see your display name, country, avatar, rating, stats, and age band — never your date of birth or password. Your email is only discoverable by someone who already knows it (that's how invitations work).

Deleting your data

Profile → Delete account removes your profile, stats, rating and friend links, and deletes your sign-in account. Encrypted chat ciphertext may persist in your friends' conversations but is unreadable without your key.
